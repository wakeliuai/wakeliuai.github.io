Rationality and Disagreement

Why Rational Individuals Diverge

In the fast-paced world of technology, especially within high-stakes environments like OpenAI's boardroom dramas, we often grapple with a puzzling question: Why do two rational individuals, presented with the exact same information, reach entirely different conclusions?

1. Divergent Preferences

Rationality is not a universal constant; it is filtered through an individual's personal values and utility functions.

2. Contextual Constraints

No two individuals operate within the same set of limitations.

3. Differing Beliefs

Even with the same data set, our interpretations are governed by our prior beliefs (Bayesian priors).

4. The Possibility of Error

Finally, we must acknowledge the humility of human fallibility. Rationality does not equate to omniscience.
